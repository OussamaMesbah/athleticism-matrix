# Source Archive: Reaction Time Meta-Analysis (Neuro-Reactive)
**Synthesized from**: Classé et al. (1997), Imamoglu et al. (2000), Parrish et al. (synthesis)

## Core Benchmarks (SRT - Simple Reaction Time)
| Discipline         | Scenario                    | Mean RT (ms)                  |
| :----------------- | :-------------------------- | :---------------------------- |
| **F1 Racing**      | Race Launch                 | ~200 (Range: 150-220)         |
| **Hockey (Pro)**   | Auditory Stimulus           | 160.0 ± 19.0                  |
| **Hockey (Pro)**   | Visual Stimulus             | 175.0 ± 14.0                  |
| **Baseball (Pro)** | Visual RT (Southern League) | 239.0                         |
| **Track (Elite)**  | 100m Start                  | 166.0 (Male) / 176.0 (Female) |
| **General Pop**    | Baseline                    | ~250.0                        |

## Extreme Performance Data
- **F1 Peak**: Some drivers record as low as 133ms in high-stakes launches.
- **Hockey Goalies**: Reacting to a 100mph slapshot from 20ft requires an effective RT of ~152ms.
- **Baseball Batters**: A 95mph pitch requires a total decision-to-contact window of ~425ms.

## Scientific Findings
- Intensive practice in high-speed sports (Baseball, Hockey) improves **Choice/Go-Nogo RT** significantly more than it improves Simple RT.
- Visual RT is generally slower than Auditory RT across all elite populations.
- Elite athletes consistently outperform general population by 40-70ms.
