# Study Summary: Hootman et al. (2007)
**Title**: Epidemiology of Collegiate Injuries for 15 Sports: Summary and Recommendations
**Source**: NCAA Injury Surveillance Data (1988-2004)

## Core Objective
Analyzed 16 years of NCAA data to establish injury rate benchmarks across 15 collegiate sports.

## Key Data: Injury Rate per 1000 Athlete-Exposures (A-Es)
| Sport                   | Game Rate (IIR) | Practice Rate (IIR) |
| :---------------------- | :-------------- | :------------------ |
| **Football (American)** | 35.9            | 9.6                 |
| **Soccer (Women)**      | 16.4            | 5.2                 |
| **Soccer (Men)**        | 18.8            | 4.3                 |
| **Wrestling**           | 26.4            | 5.7                 |
| **Basketball (Men)**    | 9.9             | 4.3                 |
| **Basketball (Women)**  | 12.1            | 4.5                 |
| **Baseball**            | 5.8             | 1.9                 |
| **Softball**            | 4.3             | 2.7                 |
| **Volleyball**          | 4.6             | 1.8                 |

## Scientific Notes
- Injury rates are significantly higher in games (13.8) vs practices (4.0).
- Preseason practice rates are significantly higher than in-season.
- American Football exhibits the highest overall trauma profile in collegiate settings.
- Women's soccer has a higher game injury rate than men's basketball.
