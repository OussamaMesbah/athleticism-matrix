Metric Glossary

🫁 Aerobic Capacity

Maximal oxygen uptake (VO2 Max) and aerobic engine efficiency. Global Peak: Elite cross-country skiers and distance runners.

💪 Force Production

Absolute force production relative to body mass. Functional power-to-weight ratio. Global Peak: Olympic weightlifters and gymnasts.

💥 Power

Force produced in minimal time (Explosiveness). Global Peak: Heavyweight boxers and track sprinters.

⚡ Speed

Top-end linear velocity across ground or water. Global Peak: 100m sprinters and speed skaters.

🔄 Agility

Rapid change of direction and deceleration poise under load. Global Peak: Soccer wingers and rugby players.

🧘 Flexibility

Range of motion across major joints without loss of stability. Global Peak: Artistic gymnasts and divers.

🧠 Resilience

Performance under extreme psychological pressure or physical danger. Global Peak: F1 drivers and Big Wave surfers.

🛡️ Durability

Resilience to physical trauma and performance maintenance through attrition. Global Peak: MMA fighters and Tour de France cyclists.

🎯 Visual Reaction

Reaction speed to external stimuli (puck, ball, signal). Global Peak: Baseball hitters and Table Tennis players.

📈 Tactical Complexity

Strategic evaluation of complex, rapidly changing scenarios. Global Peak: Tactical playmakers and racing strategists.

Scientific Methodology

Phase 1: Meta-Analysis

Since real-time biometric sensor data is rarely public across all 100 sports, we replace them with Meta-Analysis Metrics. We treat peer-reviewed physiological profiles (e.g., PLOS One, PubMed) and historical draft combine data (NFL/NBA/NHL) as our "virtual sensors."

Phase 2: Normalization & Weighting

Metrics are normalized against clinical "Human Limits" to a 0-10 scale. We then apply a 60/40 Weighted Sum (60% Physiological / 40% Technical) and calibrate the final result to a strict 0-100 Matrix Scale.

$$Score = \frac{S_{weighted} \times Mult}{Max_{theoretical}} \times 100$$

Phase 3: Objective Weighting

To ensure objectivity, we apply data-driven multipliers for METs, Biomechanical Complexity (DoF), and G-Force. Every sport also includes an Evidence Quality Rating based on the reliability of the source data (Combine vs. Inference).

Goal: Absolute physiological and statistical transparency.

Top 10 Evidence Base

  • Combat Elite: UFC PI data tracks 500+ elite fighters using force plates and Omegawave.
  • Gymnastics: Kinematic trajectory analysis for absolute force-to-weight and spatial DoF.
  • Rugby/Football: Micro-telemetry tracking of impact G-loads and repeated-sprint exhaustion.
  • F1 Racing: Sustained 160-180 BPM heart rate monitoring under 5G multi-axial neck loads.
  • Endurance Elite: Longitudinal metabolic studies of VO2 max utilization and skeletal durability.
